Last week, Co-founder and CEO Andrew Lau joined The VentureFizz Podcast for a heart to heart about his path to starting [Jellyfish](https://stgjellyfish.wpengine.com/). From his decision to study computer computer science at MIT to his early days with the [Endeca mafia](https://venturefizz.com/stories/boston/colossal-spider-web-endeca-alumni) and beyond, Andrew’s experiences and storied career have all shaped him as the leader he is today.

Andrew teamed up with co-founders Phil Braden and Dave Gourley to solve a problem they’ve all felt as technical leaders and build the leading [Engineering Management Platform](https://stgjellyfish.wpengine.com/product/) (EMP), providing complete visibility into engineering organizations, the world they do, and how they operate. We’re proud to have Andrew at the helm of Jellyfish. Check out the Andrew’s conversation with [VentureFizz](https://venturefizz.com/boston) to learn more about:

- Andrew’s decision to study computer science at MIT and how working at a handful of internships helped shape his career.
- A deep dive into his experience at Endeca, plus another startup he founded called LoopIt.
- All the details on Jellyfish and how they are enabling the business of engineering.
- Advice to entrepreneurs on idea creation and what is a viable product to build a business around.
- And so much more.
