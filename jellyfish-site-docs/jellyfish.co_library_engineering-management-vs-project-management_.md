**The main difference is that engineering management addresses the broader concerns of engineering operations, while project management focuses on the tactical aspects of executing a specific project.**

Engineering managers typically work alongside project managers to provide technical guidance and ensure that project objectives align with the broader organization’s goals.

**Software engineering management** and **project management** are in fact two distinct roles, and each comes with its own set of responsibilities and focus. While both positions involve overseeing the successful completion of projects, they differ in terms of their primary objectives and skill sets.

- Engineering management covers a broader range of responsibilities beyond individual projects. Oftentimes, engineering managers will oversee multiple projects, coordinate engineering teams, and align projects with broader organizational goals. These individuals are responsible for optimizing engineering processes and [**resource management**](https://jellyfish.co/blog/engineering-resource-planning/) while ensuring the technical success of their projects.
- A project management role is much narrower, as it focuses on the successful execution of individual projects. Project managers are responsible for planning, organizing, and overseeing project initiatives to achieve specific objectives within a predetermined set of constraints. They focus on tasks like defining the project’s scope, creating schedules, delegating tasks to the project team, managing risks, [**tracking** **project** **success** **metrics**](https://jellyfish.co/library/devops-metrics/) and ensuring project execution and deliverables.

There are several other important factors to consider when defining the roles of an engineering project manager vs project manager. Agile management, for instance, is a methodology often associated with software engineering management. Agile emphasizes flexibility, collaboration, and iterative development.

Engineering project managers who utilize [**Agile** **methodologies**](https://jellyfish.co/library/agile-metrics/) facilitate close collaboration between cross-functional teams, prioritize customer satisfaction, and adapt to changing requirements throughout the project lifecycle.

Overall, engineering management involves the strategic coordination and optimization of engineering resources across multiple different projects, while project management generally refers to a singular project.

What is Engineering Management?

## **What is Engineering Management?**

**Engineering management** **generally refers to the multidisciplinary field that combines business, management and engineering principles to effectively lead and coordinate various engineering projects and teams.**

It focuses on applying engineering knowledge and skills to help solve complex problems while considering the broader organizational perspective and business objectives.

In other words, engineering management involves the integration of technical expertise and managerial acuity to optimize engineering processes and ensure successful project outcomes.

Some responsibilities of an engineering manager include:

- **Project planning** involves defining project goals, creating a roadmap, and setting realistic timelines, often visualized and managed through project management software
- **Estimation techniques** are employed to determine the effort, time, and resources required for different project tasks.
- **Resource management** is critical, involving the [**optimal** **capacity planning**](https://jellyfish.co/blog/engineering-capacity-planning/) of team members, budget, and equipment based on project needs and individual expertise.
- **Risk management** involves identifying potential risks, developing mitigation strategies, and monitoring risks throughout the project lifecycle.
- **Quality assurance** **and quality control** activities focus on maintaining high standards in software development processes and deliverables, such as code reviews, testing, and documentation.
- **Effective team coordination** is crucial to ensure collaboration, communication, and efficient task execution within the project team.
- **Operations management** focuses more on optimizing the [**day-to-day operations**](https://jellyfish.co/library/engineering-operations/) of engineering projects. This can involve streamlining workflows, identifying bottlenecks, improving efficiency, and ensuring effective resource allocation.

What’s the Purpose of an Engineering Project Manager?

## **What’s the Purpose of an Engineering Project Manager?**

**The purpose of the engineering project manager is to oversee the entire project lifecycle from inception to completion.** Their primary responsibilities typically include defining project outlines, creating work breakdown structures, establishing project schedules with clear milestones, allocating resources, managing budgets, and monitoring project progress against those milestones.

Engineering project management, more broadly, is the systematic application of project management principles and techniques to plan, execute, and control engineering projects. It involves coordinating resources, tasks, and stakeholders to ensure successful project outcomes within the constraints of time, cost, and quality.

What Does it Take to Become a Great Engineering Project Manager?

## **What Does it Take to Become a Great Engineering Project Manager?**

It’s a demanding role that requires a special blend of the following:

- **Technical know-how:** technical skills within the broader engineering discipline to understand the key project requirements and assess all technical aspects.
- **Project management** **expertise:** Additionally, project management professionals must be proficient in project management methodologies, tools, and techniques to effectively plan and oversee engineering projects. Certifications like the PMP are often valued.
- **Planning and analytical skills:** It’s crucial for engineering project managers to allocate resources wisely to hit those key project objectives.
- **Soft skills** **:** Soft skills also matter! Engineering program managers must combine strong leadership and communication skills with well-honed project management skills encompassing planning, risk mitigation, and stakeholder
- **Sharp thinking:** Problem-solving, critical thinking, and quick decision-making skills are also essential for identifying and addressing any project issues promptly.
