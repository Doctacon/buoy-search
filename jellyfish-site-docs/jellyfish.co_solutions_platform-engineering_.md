## What Platform Teams Can Do with Jellyfish

[Tour the Product](https://tour.jellyfish.co/embed/xrg7l62twr)

![](https://jellyfish.co/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/feature-devops-metrics-v2.webp)

### Make Metrics Actionable

Explore DORA, flow, and custom metrics with filters, overlays, and time-series diagnostics across teams, repos, and services.

![](https://jellyfish.co/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/feature-devexs-metrics.webp)

### Improve DevEx with Proof

Combine developer feedback with system signals and benchmarks to prioritize improvements and show measurable gains in experience over time.

![](https://jellyfish.co/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/feature-ai-impact.webp)

### Operationalize AI Usage

Guide AI tool rollout by seeing where usage helps or hurts, enabling best practices, and scaling what works across teams and codebases.

![](https://jellyfish.co/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/feature-benchmarks.webp)

### Set the Bar with Benchmarks

Compare teams and the broader org against industry peers to understand maturity, efficiency, and where platform investments matter most.

![](https://jellyfish.co/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/feature-lifecycle-explorer.webp)

### Fix Broken Workflows

Trace work from intake to deployment to uncover bottlenecks, handoffs, and delays, and connect them directly to delivery and DevEx outcomes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

How does Jellyfish support platform engineering teams?

Jellyfish gives platform engineering teams system-level visibility into how work flows across tools, teams, and pipelines. By unifying metrics, developer experience data, AI usage, and workflow analysis, Jellyfish helps platform teams identify bottlenecks, prioritize improvements, and prove the impact of their work.

What metrics should platform engineering teams track?

Platform engineering teams typically track DORA metrics, flow metrics, cycle time, deployment health, developer experience metrics, and operational signals across tools and pipelines. Jellyfish unifies these metrics in a single data model, making it easier to diagnose issues and guide platform improvements.

How can platform teams measure developer experience (DevEx)?

Developer experience is measured by combining qualitative feedback with system metrics such as build latency, cognitive load, context switching, and workflow friction. Jellyfish connects DevEx surveys with engineering metrics so platform teams can identify friction, prioritize improvements, and track DevEx over time.

How is Jellyfish different from internal dashboards?

Jellyfish provides a normalized data model, inference engine, and benchmarks, so platform teams don’t have to maintain scripts or reconcile metrics manually.

Does Jellyfish require developer time tracking?

No. Jellyfish infers work automatically from event-level signals without interrupting developer workflows.
